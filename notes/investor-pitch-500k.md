# Investor Pitch — Why $500K?

## The 30-second answer

> Grocery is a $800B+ decision moment with almost zero real-time intelligence. 50M people use scanner apps for scores — but nobody captures *why* shoppers reject products at the shelf. We're building the decision layer for cutting shoppers first, then selling aggregated rejection intelligence to brands. $500K buys us proof that people change their cart every week — that's the only thing that makes this a company.

---

## What $500K buys

| Use of funds | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Prove retention** | Scanner apps die at week 2. If we hit 25%+ weekly use in one wedge, we have something Yuka never proved. |
| **Own the beachhead data** | Protein bars + yogurt for US cutting shoppers — curated, high-confidence, not raw OFF garbage |
| **Ship comparison + cart audit** | The feature that makes trip 3 different from trip 1 |
| **First B2B design partners** | 1–2 brands paying for "why you lost to Quest on the shelf" — not ads, not score boosts |

---

## The bull case

1. **Wedge is real.** Cutting shoppers already scan, compare, and agonize over labels. They have money, repeat weekly, and share on fitness TikTok.

2. **Timing.** AI makes 5-second personalized verdicts possible. OFF + mobile OCR makes data cheaper than 5 years ago.

3. **The moat isn't the score — it's the "why."** Nielsen tells brands what sold. Nobody tells them *"I almost bought it but the sugar was too high and the ingredients looked fake"* at scale, at the moment of decision.

4. **Trust architecture is the business model.** No paid rankings = consumer trust = the only asset brands will pay to understand without destroying.

5. **Exit paths exist.** CPG insights (Circana, Mintel), retail media (Kroger 84.51°), or acqui-hire by Instacart/MyFitnessPal/Whoop-adjacent health apps that want grocery intent data.

---

## Pushback answers

**"Isn't this just Yuka?"**
> Yuka scores products. We score decisions for a specific goal and capture why people reject. Comparison mode + cart audit + structured rejection data — that's the product. The score is table stakes.

**"Scanner apps don't retain."**
> Correct. That's exactly what $500K tests. We're not raising to build features — we're raising to prove weekly grocery behavior change in one persona. If we fail, you lose $500K. If we pass, you have a wedge into a $50B+ CPG insights market.

**"Why won't Yuka add a button?"**
> They could. They haven't in 10 years. Their model is consumer subscription + ads, not moment-of-decision qual at scale. We're building for the gap, not the score.

**"B2B is years away."**
> Yes. $500K is not for B2B. It's for consumer retention proof. B2B is the Series A story if W2 retention hits 25–35%.

## The close

> You're not investing in an AI grocery app. You're investing in whether we can become the **trusted decision layer** for one high-intent shopper segment — and whether that creates a data asset brands can't get anywhere else. $500K gets us to that answer in 12 months. Without weekly retention, this is a feature. With it, it's infrastructure.
